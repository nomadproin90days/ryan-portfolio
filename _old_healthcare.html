The Problem

A compounding pharmacy operates as both a compounding pharmacy and an IV infusion clinic. They needed a system that could handle two completely different workflows: a B2B provider acquisition pipeline (selling to doctors) and a B2C patient fulfillment pipeline (processing prescriptions from intake to delivery). Everything had to be HIPAA-compliant, support multiple languages for their diverse patient base, and integrate with their existing pharmacy management system (Pioneer Rx).

What I Built

  • Patient Intake Portal
    Multi-language patient intake forms (English, Korean, Vietnamese) built in Next.js. Collects medical history, insurance info, and consent - all HIPAA-compliant with encrypted data handling.
  • Provider B2B Sales Pipeline
    Lead to Portal Activated in 6 stages. NPI credentialing, contract signing, portal activation with SLA tracking. Automated onboarding sequences for new prescribers.
  • Rx Fulfillment Pipeline
    From Rx Received to Complete. Pharmacist review gates, payment processing via webhooks, compounding SLAs for sterile vs non-sterile compounds, shipping with carrier tracking.
  • Auto-Refill System
    Automated refill triggers based on days supply, remaining refills, and Rx expiration dates. Stops prompting when refills hit zero or prescriptions expire.
  • GHL Automation Blueprint
    Complete GHL workflow architecture: 15+ custom fields per contact type, provider vs patient tag taxonomy, AI-powered call summaries, escalation SLAs, and legacy fax exception handling.
  • Session Reporting
    Post-visit documentation system for infusion sessions. Clinical workflow tracking, session reports, and provider referral management.
  • Payment & Webhook Infrastructure
    Payment processing webhooks, Pioneer Rx integration endpoints, promo code claiming and tracking APIs. All built with audit trails for HIPAA compliance.
  • Cybersecurity Lockdown
    Full security audit and lockdown. Protection manifests, security libraries, and hardened infrastructure to meet healthcare compliance requirements.

Compliance

HIPAA

Patient data encrypted at rest and in transit. Consent tracking with signed dates. Audit trails on every data access. HIPAA-aware architecture from the ground up.

Multi-Language

Full localization system supporting English, Korean, and Vietnamese across all patient-facing pages: intake forms, session reports, referrals, and the patient hub.

Payment Security

Webhook-based payment processing with verification. Consent must be signed before payment triggers. Revenue analytics with payment date tracking.

Data Protection

Protection manifests, security libraries, and a full cybersecurity lockdown protocol. Every endpoint hardened against unauthorized access.

Results

  • Zero to Launch
    Took the pharmacy from no digital infrastructure to a fully operational HIPAA-compliant system — patient intake, prescription fulfillment, provider sales pipeline, and payment processing — all built from scratch.
  • Dual Pipeline
    Unified two separate business models (B2B provider acquisition and B2C patient fulfillment) into a single CRM architecture with shared contact records and distinct workflow paths.
  • Multi-Language Access
    Full i18n localization across all patient-facing surfaces — English, Korean, and Vietnamese — expanding the clinic's reach to underserved patient populations in their region.
  • Refill Automation
    Automated prescription refill triggers based on days supply, remaining refills, and expiration dates — eliminating manual tracking and reducing missed refill windows.
